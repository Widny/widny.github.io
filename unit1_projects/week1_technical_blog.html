I do not know what I would do without Audible. Up until collge, I was an avid reader of fiction books. For me, there was nothing better than a good science fiction, fantasy, or an adventure novel that can take me far away into another world; or an action book that keeps my heart racing until you get to the last word. I couldn't wait for a Friday or Saturday to come where I can pick up a book and read it all the way through, ignoring my growling stomache because I did not want to put the book down. Those were the really good books. When I was in collge, I began reading non fiction biographes and historical events.

It all changed when I started working, especially at my last job. I read less as I started working more hours. Work took over my life and my favorite hobbies suchs as reading. I was miserable. I longed for a good story, but time was my antagonist.

To get back to reviewing the Audible website (sorry for the nostalgic rant). I signed up for Audible last year. Boy oh boy, was it a life saver. The website is very simple, which is my favorite aesthetic (less is more). The focus is on the product, as it should be. It is very easy to browse for books. You can filter your search for specific genres. Or you can search their best-sellers list, or go through their section of new books. One feature I really like is that they recoomend books based on my past purchases. if you rate your purchases, it will improve their recommendations to you. Once you have chosen the book, the purchasing process is as simple as adding your purchase(s) to your cart, placing the order, downloading the book which can be synced to any device and that's it.

When you go to the website and click on "kitchen", there are tns of pictures you can scroll through. on the left there is a sidebar where the user can filter their search to their design aesthetic, location, pricing, and etc.
